General EB-5 Program

What is the EB-5 visa program?

The EB-5 Immigrant Investor Program is a United States visa program created by Congress in 1990 to stimulate the US economy through job creation and capital investment by foreign investors. It provides a pathway to permanent residency (green card) for investors and their immediate family members who invest in qualifying US businesses.

How much do I need to invest?

The minimum investment amounts are:

  • $800,000 USD - For investments in Targeted Employment Areas (TEA), which are rural areas or areas with high unemployment
  • $1,050,000 USD - For investments in standard (non-TEA) areas

Most investors choose TEA projects to benefit from the lower investment threshold.

Who is eligible for the EB-5 program?

To be eligible for EB-5, you must:

  • Have the required investment capital ($800,000 or $1,050,000)
  • Prove the lawful source of your investment funds
  • Invest in a USCIS-approved regional center or qualifying direct business
  • Create or preserve at least 10 full-time jobs for US workers
  • Not have any disqualifying criminal history
  • Pass medical examinations

There are no age, education, language, or business experience requirements.

How long does the EB-5 process take?

The complete EB-5 process typically takes 4-6 years from initial investment to permanent green card:

  • I-526E Petition: 24-36 months for USCIS processing
  • Consular Processing/AOS: 6-12 months
  • Conditional Green Card: Valid for 2 years
  • I-829 Petition: 18-24 months to remove conditions

Processing times vary based on USCIS workload and country-specific visa availability.

Can my family members also get green cards?

Yes! The EB-5 visa includes your immediate family members:

  • Spouse: Your legally married spouse receives a green card and can work anywhere in the US
  • Children: Unmarried children under 21 years old at the time of I-526E filing receive green cards and can attend US schools without international student restrictions

Important: Children must be under 21 when the I-526E petition is filed to be included. Age-out protection may apply in certain circumstances.

Investment & Financial

What is "source of funds" and why is it important?

Source of funds documentation proves that your investment capital was obtained through lawful means. USCIS requires comprehensive evidence showing:

  • How you earned or acquired the investment funds
  • Tax compliance on income used for investment
  • Legal transfer of funds from source country to US
  • Complete paper trail of fund movement

Acceptable sources include: business income, salary, property sales, inheritance, gifts, loans (with proper documentation), and investment returns.

Will I get my investment back?

EB-5 investments are "at-risk" capital, meaning there is no guarantee of return. However:

  • Most regional center projects are structured to return capital after 5-7 years
  • Historical return rates vary by project type and market conditions
  • Your investment must remain "at risk" throughout the conditional green card period
  • Some projects offer preferred returns or interest payments

Important: Focus on immigration goals first, investment returns second. Choose projects with strong job creation models and reputable developers.

What are the total costs beyond the investment amount?

In addition to the $800,000 or $1,050,000 investment, expect these costs:

  • USCIS Filing Fees: $11,160 (I-526E) + $3,835 (I-829)
  • Immigration Attorney Fees: $15,000 - $30,000
  • Regional Center Admin Fee: $50,000 - $80,000
  • Consulting Fees: Varies by service package
  • Document Translation/Notarization: $2,000 - $5,000
  • Medical Exams: $500 - $1,000 per person

Total Additional Costs: Approximately $80,000 - $130,000 beyond the investment amount.

Can I use borrowed funds for my EB-5 investment?

Yes, you can use borrowed funds, but you must demonstrate:

  • The loan is secured by your own assets (not the EB-5 project)
  • You are personally liable for repayment
  • The collateral was acquired through lawful means
  • Complete documentation of the loan agreement

The source of funds used to acquire the collateral must also be documented.

Regional Centers & Projects

What is a regional center?

A regional center is a USCIS-designated entity that sponsors EB-5 investment projects. Benefits include:

  • Indirect Job Creation: Can count indirect and induced jobs, not just direct employment
  • No Management Required: Investors are passive; no day-to-day involvement needed
  • Diversified Projects: Access to large-scale commercial developments
  • Professional Management: Experienced teams handle project execution

Over 90% of EB-5 investors choose regional center projects for these advantages.

How do I choose a regional center project?

We help you evaluate projects based on:

  • Developer Track Record: History of completed projects and EB-5 approvals
  • Job Creation Model: Conservative projections with safety margin
  • Capital Stack: Healthy mix of EB-5 and senior debt
  • Project Stage: Preference for shovel-ready or under-construction projects
  • Exit Strategy: Clear plan for capital return
  • Regional Center Reputation: USCIS approval history and compliance record
What types of projects are available?

Common EB-5 project types include:

  • Real Estate: Hotels, mixed-use developments, residential projects
  • Infrastructure: Transportation, energy, public works
  • Healthcare: Hospitals, senior living, medical facilities
  • Manufacturing: Production facilities, industrial parks
  • Technology: Data centers, tech campuses

We focus on projects with strong fundamentals and proven job creation models.

Process & Timeline

What is the I-526E petition?

The I-526E (Immigrant Petition by Regional Center Investor) is the first major step in the EB-5 process. It demonstrates:

  • You have invested or are actively investing the required capital
  • Your funds were obtained lawfully
  • The investment will create at least 10 jobs
  • The regional center and project are USCIS-approved

Approval of I-526E allows you to proceed with visa processing and obtain conditional green cards.

What happens after I-526E approval?

After I-526E approval, you have two options:

  • Consular Processing: If outside the US, apply for immigrant visa at US Embassy/Consulate in your home country
  • Adjustment of Status: If already in the US on valid status, apply to adjust to permanent resident status

Upon approval, you and your family receive conditional green cards valid for 2 years.

What is the I-829 petition?

The I-829 (Petition by Investor to Remove Conditions) is filed 90 days before your conditional green card expires. It demonstrates:

  • Your investment capital remained at risk throughout the conditional period
  • The required 10 jobs were created or will be created
  • You maintained your investment in the project

Upon I-829 approval, you receive permanent, unrestricted green cards with no expiration conditions.

Can I work in the US while on a conditional green card?

Yes! Conditional green card holders have the same work rights as permanent residents:

  • Work for any employer in any field
  • Start your own business
  • Live anywhere in the United States
  • Travel freely in and out of the US
  • Access to Social Security benefits (after qualifying period)

The only restriction is that you must maintain your EB-5 investment until I-829 approval.
